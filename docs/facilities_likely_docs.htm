Description:

 

Abstract:

This dataset provides information on nitrogen (N) and phosphorus (P) discharge from facility monitoring reports (2014) and corresponding N and P limits from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s (EPA) Discharge Monitoring Report (DMR) Pollutant Loading Tool. Records are provided only for industrial and treatment facilities with individual National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permits that, based on their operation, may be discharging nitrogen and phosphorus.

 

Purpose:

Consider the information in each permit; where there is monitoring data and/or limits, use this information to generate the load reductions occurring. Where this information is not part of the information on the permit, look towards other data sources that can fill in these blanks.  Also, consider nitrate discharges to high density drinking water sources.

 

 

Supplemental_Information:

 

The Nitrogen and Phosphorus Pollution Data Access Tool (NPDAT) provides data for use in both spreadsheets and geospatial tools, such Geographic Information Systems (GIS).  For this reason, the attribute and geospatial data are served as separate downloadable files.  This single metadata document describes both the attribute and geospatial data provided for this data layer.  Note that the this dataset only includes 2014 data. Additional data is available at: http://cfpub.epa.gov/dmr/.

 

Data Sources:

 

The DMR Pollutant Loading Tool uses discharge monitoring and permit data from Permit Compliance System (PCS) and Integrated Compliance Information System for the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (ICIS-NPDES). PCS and ICIS-NPDES are databases that automate entering, updating, and retrieving discharge monitoring and permit data.

 

Dischargers submit discharge monitoring data to their permitting authority using DMR forms. The permitting authority then enters these data into PCS or ICIS-NPDES and checks whether the discharger is in compliance with the NPDES permit requirements. Facilities report pollutant discharge monitoring data in their DMR as mass-based quantities (e.g., pounds per day) and/or concentrations (e.g., mg/L); however, discharges are reported using a wide variety of units.

 

For more information about PCS and ICIS, go to: http://www.epa.gov/enviro/facts/pcs-icis/index.html

Other data sources used by the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool include:

·         Watershed Assessment Tracking and Environmental ResultS (WATERS) web services for retrieving information about receiving waterbodies (available online at http://www.epa.gov/waters/geoservices/index.html); and

·         Facility Registry System (FRS) for providing facility location information, such as latitude/longitude coordinates (available online at http://www.epa.gov/enviro/html/facility.html).

 

The following describes limitations of the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool data as they apply to this dataset:

 

·         The Loading Tool contains information for industrial and municipal point source dischargers. Other types of Clean Water Act releases that are not available in the Loading Tool include:

 

·         Wastewater releases from industrial facilities that are connected to a publicly-owned treatment works (POTW) sewerage system, regulated through the CWA Pretreatment Program;

 

·         Biosolids monitoring data, regulated through the CWA Biosolids Program;

 

·         Discharges related to wet-weather events, such as stormwater from municipal separate storm sewer systems (MS4s), stormwater from industrial facilities, discharges from construction activities, combined sewer overflows, sanitary sewer overflows, and concentrated animal feeding operations (CAFOs).

 

·         PCS and ICIS-NPDES only include information about discharges of pollutants that a facility is required by permit to monitor; facilities are not required to monitor or report all pollutants they actually discharge. Permitting authorities are not required to submit DMR data to PCS or ICIS-NPDES for facilities that they classify as "minor" dischargers.  For purposes of this dataset, EPA identified all sewage treatment plants as “likely to discharge N/P”, regardless of the availability of permit or DMR in PCS and ICIS-NPDES

 

·         DMR data may be entered into the PCS or ICIS-NPDES database manually, which can lead to data-entry errors. EPA has verified the accuracy of the calculations, and has conducted a preliminary review of the tool output to verify the accuracy of the underlying DMR data used for the calculations. However, EPA's review of the underlying data has focused on the highest discharges and included a limited number of facilities. Due to the volume of data, additional errors exist.

 

·         Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) codes used to identify a facility’s industrial category are not included for all facilities; therefore, some facilities cannot be classified by industry, and cannot be linked to EPA’s typical pollutant concentrations (TPC) data. In addition, although multiple SIC codes may apply to a facility, the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool only uses the primary SIC code to classify facilities by industry.  Note that the TPC table was used to identify facilities in the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool data extract, using their primary SIC code, that are likely to discharge nitrogen or phosphorus compounds.

 

·         FRS, PCS, and ICIS-NPDES do not have latitude and longitude coordinates for all facilities.

 

·         The WATERS web services do not provide watershed information for all facilities in the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool database.

 

Additional documentation about the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool can be found online at http://cfpub.epa.gov/dmr/usersguide.cfm.

Use_Constraints:

In using these data, consider the following limitations:

 

**Point sources are only required to report nitrogen and phosphorus in their effluent if there is a limit or monitoring requirement for these pollutants in their permit.

 

**In administration and oversight of the NPDES program, EPA has divided NPDES permit holders as either “major” or “non-major” (a.k.a. “minor”) dischargers. For example, EPA identifies municipal wastewater treatment plants or POTWs that discharge 1 million gallons per day or more as “major” dischargers. EPA has placed greater priority on major facilities, and has required authorized states to provide more information about the compliance status of these dischargers. Specifically, EPA policy requires the permitting authority to enter DMR information from “major discharges” into one of two EPA databases: PCS or ICIS-NPDES. Information for "minor" facilities in these data systems is incomplete.

Lineage:

 

Process_Step:

 

Process_Description:Obtain custom extract from PCS and ICIS-NPDES databases through the DMR Pollutant Loading Tool. The custom extract identified only facilities located in the lower 48 states (conterminous US).  “Facilities likely to discharge N or P to water” were identified using the following requirements:

·         Permit has discharge data for aggregated nitrogen or aggregated phosphorus for 2014;

·         Permit has limits for nitrogen and/or phosphorus compounds;

·         Permit has monitoring requirements for nitrogen and/or phosphorus compounds;

·         Facility is a publicly-owned treatment works (POTW);

·         Permit has a primary SIC code that links to SIC codes in EPA’s typical pollutant concentrations (TPC) table that has typical concentrations for nitrogen and/or phosphorus compounds.

Process_Description:

Spatially enable the attribute table using the Longitude and Latitude columns. Values were given in NAD83.
